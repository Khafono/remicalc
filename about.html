About This App

This project was born out of simple observation — watching Kenya Prisons Service officers tirelessly working with an age-old manual system, performing the same complex calculations day in and day out. I took time to speak with them, to understand their challenges, and to suggest ways the process could be modernized.

It took tens of days to fully understand the entire concept, and in the process I distilled it into a 9-Point Gold Standard — a practical, precise guide to ensure accuracy, consistency, and fairness in sentence computation.

I could not resist the challenge to leave a positive mark at Kiambu Main Prison and, hopefully, across the entire Kenya Prisons Service. After navigating through many misconceptions and assumptions… I finally scored it.

The 9-Point Gold Standard

  1. When computing LPD and Remission, treat a month as 30 days and a year as 12 months.
  2. When computing EPD, especially when borrowing, borrow an actual calendar month from the previous full month.
  3. For decimals ≥ 0.5, round up. For decimals < 0.5, round down.
  4. 1–30 days → no remission.
  5. 31–44 days → remission is the number of days above 30.
  6. From 45 days → remission is exactly one third.
  7. LPD = (Sentence Date + Sentence) − 1 day. (The day of sentencing counts as served.)
  8. Remission is exactly one third. Get the nearest number with a perfect third; remaining units should be converted down (years → months → days).
  9. EPD = (LPD − Remission) + 1 day. This guide must be followed without exception.
